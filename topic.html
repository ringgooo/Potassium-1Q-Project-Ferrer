ISSUES

Filipino farmers face consistently low incomes due to limited acces to credit, markets, and modern technology, high cost of inputs like fertilizer, significant post harvest losses, and exploitation by middlemen.

The philippines, as a net fertilizer importer, is vulnerable to rising global prices driven by the COVID-19 pandemic, supply disruptions, and the Russia-Ukraine conflict (Food and Agriculture Organization [FAO], 2022).

Filipino farmers face issues from a general lack of government support, including insufficient funding, a failure to provide sufficient acces to formal credit and insurance, inadequate infrastructure, and weak policies that fail to adress issues like land tenure and climate vulnerability.
